Background information

Various types of terrestrial habitat exist Terrestrial habitats are recognized mainly by their vegetations which are determined by rainfall and temperature. Terrestrial habitats include: Marsh, Forest, grassland, and arid land.
Marsh: This is a low land habitat featuring the growth of grasses and shrubs and the presence of flood. A marsh can be considered to be a habitat that exist in between as we depart from aquatic to terrestrial habitat.

Characteristic of a marsh

  1. Most of the time the land is flooded
  2. Lowland
  3. The soil is usually soft, wet and water logged
  4. High relative humidity in the atmosphere over the habitat.
  5. Sometimes the water bodies contain decaying organic matters, this affects oxygen content of the habitat and also change its chemical composition.

Formation and Types of Marshes

Marshes can be formed by the following ways:
  1. Overflowing of river banks during rainy season coupled with the heavy rainfall makes them results into a marsh habitat.
  2. At hightide, sea water overflows into the lagoon and may fill it to overflowing. The overflowing water may eventually flood.. The surrounding low land resulting to a marsh habitat.
  3. Rivers also flow into lagoons as a result of rainfall during rainy season. This addition of large volume of river water to the lagoon can cause the overflowing of the lagoon thus giving rise to a marsh habitat at the surrounding or adjoining low land.
  4. The partial filling up of a lake by debris. A marsh can be either a salt water marsh or a fresh water marsh.

FOREST: A forest is a biome which consists mostly of trees. The main forest biomes include: Tropical rainforests, temperate deciduous forests and the coniferous forests. In Nigeria the major type of forest is the rain forest. It is influenced by rainfall, temperature and humidity which are abiotic factors.

CHARACTERISTICS OF THE RAINFOREST

  1. Mesophyte mostly of tree species with broad leaves
  2. Storeys or layers pattern of vegetation arrangement.
  3. There use to be dim light on the forest floor due to foliage cover or canopy of trees.
  4. Little vegetation at the forest floor
  5. Presence of many climbing plants and epiphytes.

Five main vegetative layers or strata are identified in a rain forest

  1. Ground floor layer of non-woody herbaceous plants, lichens, fungi, mosses and liverworts.
  2. Lower layer which consists of various shade loving dwarf trees, shrubs and palms.
  3. A middle layer of smaller trees which have elongated narrow crowns.
  4. Upper layer which consists of tall trees with their crowns forming a continuous canopy or foliage cover.
  5. Emergent trees which occur at intervals high or above the canopy

A GRASS LAND: Biome in which grasses are dominant is called grassland. Rainfall and temperature mostly determine the vegetation of a grassland.

Characteristics of grassland

  1. Rainfall ranges from moderate to low.
  2. There use to be intense sunshine
  3. During dry season, bush fire usually occur
  4. The soil use to be sandy.

Types of grasslands

There is tropical grassland and also a temperate grassland
The tropical grassland is divided into: southern guinea savanna, Northern guinea savannah, sahel savanna and derived savannah

ARID LANDS

Lands where water is very scarce and difficult to obtain are called arid lands: it is the driest habitat that exist. Major arid lands include: tropical deserts, warm temperate deserts and the arctic tundra.

Characteristics of a tropical desert

  1. Scarcity of water (rainfall is scarce)
  2. High temperature during the day and low temperature at night;
  3. Rocky or sandy soil texture
  4. Strong dry wind which erodes the land
  5. There is intense sunshine due to little vegetation.

Characteristic of warm temperate desert

  1. Winter temperature that is usually below freezing point with the exception of the southern continents.
  2. Scarcity of rainfall, which usually occurs in summer but fall every year. Characteristic of Artic tundra
  3. Summer is very short (about 3 month) with observable longer days.
  4. Very low temperature of 100 c in summer.
  5. Few centimeters part of soil is frozen
  6. Muddy water logged soil is present
  7. Winter is long, dark and is very cold below –150C
